CONVERGENCE LATTICE

Agent-Driven Value Flows, Self-Serve Infrastructure, and the Topology of Semi-Multiplanetary Coordination

Overview

This work presents the Convergence Lattice — a recursive, multi-scale structure that quietly emerges when autonomous agents, programmable value flows, self-serve abstraction layers, and distributed edge hardware begin to interact under the unforgiving constraints of real physics and latency.

What starts as a messy tangle of overlapping systems — computational, financial, and physical substrates that barely talk to each other — slowly begins to organize itself. Density increases. Patterns repeat across scales. Coordination overhead, that eternal tax on complexity, starts to compress in surprising ways. At certain thresholds the lattice shifts phase: what once felt chaotic suddenly feels almost inevitable.

Is it possible that adding more agents and more connections doesn’t always make the system harder to run — but, under the right topology, actually makes it easier?

Key Concepts

Substrate Formation — The foundational layers that enable autonomous coordination.

The Recursive Lattice — A structure that repeats across scales.

Programmable Value Flows — Value transfer at the speed of computation.

Edge Co-Design & Semi-Multiplanetary Topology — Autonomy as a structural requirement.
